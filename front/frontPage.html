Environmental Impact

Soil Pollution

By improperly disposing of e-waste in landfills or elsewhere, we risk contaminating the ground indefinitely with heavy metals and flame retardants. Contaminated soil can remain toxic for years– even decades– affecting crops and wildlife that eventually soak up these chemicals in their systems.

Water Pollution

Dumping e-waste in landfills can form leachates that seep into groundwater supplies and lead to rivers, lakes, and oceans. These leachates contain hazardous contaminants like lead, mercy, or plastics that cause acidification in fresh or salt waters. This acidification results in dead marine life, declining biodiversity, and destroyed ecosystems.

Air Pollution

Burning e-waste releases toxic chemicals (mercury, lead, cadmium, flame retardants, etc) that can travel thousands of miles away and chronically pollute organisms and ecosystems far from these landfills. Additionally, vast amounts of carbon dioxide is released, contributing to global emissions. These fine particles slowly accumulate over time, resulting in serious health disorders in organisms.

Human Health

Toxins contained in e-waste adversely affect human health, targeting the human nervous and reproductive systems and major organs like the heart, liver, and kidneys. The accumulation of these toxins can lead to birth defects, disease, and numerous other health complications.

Resource Depletion

Electronics involve rare earth metals such as gold and copper which could be repurposed in new electronics. Rare earth metals are scarce, and needlessly discarding them in landfills when they can be repurposed contributes to resource depletion. In turn, this increases demand for such metals and incites mining operations that adversely affect the environment.

Overcrowding Landfills

As the demand for electronics continues to rise, the need for sustainable disposal methods becomes more urgent. Designating space for larger landfills further destroys numerous habitats, creating inhospitable land filled with materials that could instead be reused.

BOOST LONGEVITY

The average U.S. household disposes of 176 pounds of electronics per year! That's 400 iPhones worth of e-waste. Most people don’t need to upgrade their devices as often as they do, opting for a newer model with slightly better features. This increases the production amount of e-waste significantly. By boosting the longevity of your devices and choosing not to upcycle, you will not only be able to save money in the long run but also save the environment!

CHOOSE RECYCLING

Although the US federal government banned dumping e-waste in landfills in 1976 under the RCRA, only a small fraction of e-waste gets effectively recycled while the rest (85%) still ends up in landfills. From there, chemicals from e-waste often contaminate the ground, water, and air when incinerated. E-waste has many negative effects on the world and on human health. We must properly recycle to protect the environment from e-waste pollution. Recycle your old computers and computer components at E-Waste Recycling Centers to do your part.
